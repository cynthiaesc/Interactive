el nido/the nest

installation, urbanism

Working under the guidance of San Francisco LMNOP Design firm who is leading La Cocina Municipal Market Place Project with the goal to make LCMMP an extension of the local family community from the Tenderloin district. The final task was to design a mechanism (tool) that generates engagement from the Tenderloin children and their families within the Municipal Market Place space. For this proposal LCMMP becomes El Nido/The Nest, a place that provides shelter and food and the birds will be the incoming visitors and vendors from different places (cultures) which activate the nest. Utilizing the walls of the facility and cardstock bird-forms, the goal of this project is to keep track of people who contribute and build the LCMMP (vendors, children, community) over time and generate a sense of community and pride among participants.
